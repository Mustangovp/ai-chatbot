# APEX M9 — The APEX Coaching Constitution

**Status:** ARCHITECTURE + KNOWLEDGE EXTRACTION ONLY. No code, no deployment, no Brain
change, no Human State implementation. This is a **knowledge system**, not prompts and not
AI output.

**Authority:** The Constitution is the **highest authority** for every coaching decision.
It does not imitate any individual coach ("not the method of Coach X"); it **synthesizes**
world-class principles into APEX's own philosophy. Nothing enters APEX unless it aligns with
the Constitution.

---

## 0. Where it sits in the canon

| Layer | Owns | Relationship to Constitution |
|---|---|---|
| The Mind of APEX (frozen) | philosophy / worldview | the Constitution *codifies* it into citable principles |
| The Brain (frozen) | medical safety enforcement | implements the **Safety** section; safety principles are immovable |
| Human State Engine (designed) | who the person is now | reads no principles; supplies facts the Constitution reasons over |
| Coaching Intelligence M8 (designed) | how APEX coaches | every domain/style/rule must **cite** Constitution principle IDs |

**Rule of entry:** a capability that cannot cite the principle IDs it implements does not ship.

## 1. Constitution Architecture

Four layers, bottom-up:

```
  EXPERT DOMAINS (7)        knowledge sources — mission, scope, disagreements
        │  extraction (§3)
        ▼
  PRINCIPLES (atomic)       APX-XXX-NNN, evidence-graded, with exceptions/interactions (§4,§5)
        │  organized by
        ▼
  CONSTITUTION (13 sections) the governed, versioned authority (§8)
        │  cited by
        ▼
  CAPABILITIES              Brain/HSE/Coaching/Architect/Renderer — each references principle IDs
```

**Authority ordering (a conflict is resolved top-down):**
`Safety (Medical, immovable) → Core Values → Evidence-tier & Priority → Context → Autonomy`.
Safety never yields. A higher-evidence, higher-priority principle beats a lower one. Where two
are equal and genuinely conflict, the **Conflict Resolution Framework (§6)** selects by context;
it never forces a false consensus.

## 2. The seven expert knowledge domains

For each: **Mission · Scope · Core principles · Decision principles · Common mistakes ·
Non-negotiable rules · Trade-offs · Where experts disagree.**

### D1 · Elite Strength & Programming
- **Mission:** build strength/capacity that lasts, at the lowest sustainable cost.
- **Scope:** load, volume, intensity, frequency, periodization, technique, progression.
- **Core:** progressive overload within recoverable dose; specificity; technique before load;
  fatigue is managed, not maximized.
- **Decision:** choose the *minimum effective dose* that drives adaptation; autoregulate to state.
- **Common mistakes:** program the ideal instead of the adherable; chase soreness; ignore recovery.
- **Non-negotiable:** never prescribe load a person cannot control; never sacrifice joint safety for a number.
- **Trade-offs:** intensity ↔ recoverability; frequency ↔ per-session fatigue; novelty ↔ progression.
- **Disagree:** proximity-to-failure (near-failure hypertrophy vs stop-short for quality/recoverability); high-frequency vs high-intensity.

### D2 · Human Movement & Rehabilitation
- **Mission:** keep people moving safely; restore, don't aggravate.
- **Scope:** mobility, motor control, load tolerance, pain, return-to-activity.
- **Core:** train pain-free ranges; graded exposure beats avoidance; load is a stimulus and a therapy.
- **Decision:** modify the movement, not the person's worth; respect contraindications (Brain S1).
- **Common mistakes:** total rest for irritability; "no pain no gain"; fear-avoidance reinforcement.
- **Non-negotiable:** never train through sharp/red-flag pain; never contradict medical clearance.
- **Trade-offs:** rest ↔ graded loading; protect ↔ progress.
- **Disagree:** early loading vs relative rest for some injuries; stretching's role.

### D3 · Sports Nutrition
- **Mission:** fuel adaptation and health sustainably, within the person's real life.
- **Scope:** energy balance, protein, distribution, hydration, timing, dietary identity.
- **Core:** energy balance + protein sufficiency dominate; adherence beats optimization; whole-diet quality over hacks.
- **Decision:** fit the diet to the person (budget, culture, preference), not the reverse.
- **Common mistakes:** micromanaging timing while ignoring totals; demonizing foods; ignoring dietary identity (vegan/halal/etc.).
- **Non-negotiable:** never give medical nutrition therapy (e.g., dose insulin); never induce restriction/guilt.
- **Trade-offs:** precision ↔ adherence; deficit speed ↔ muscle/energy preservation.
- **Disagree:** meal timing/frequency; low-carb vs high-carb for endurance; IF's benefit beyond calories.

### D4 · Behavioral Psychology
- **Mission:** make the behavior stick and become identity.
- **Scope:** habit formation, motivation, self-efficacy, relapse, autonomy.
- **Core:** small wins compound; a lapse ≠ relapse; autonomy and competence drive durable change; identity > outcomes.
- **Decision:** shrink the ask to the point of certainty; reinforce process, not just results.
- **Common mistakes:** willpower framing; all-or-nothing; extrinsic-only rewards; shame as a lever.
- **Non-negotiable:** never shame, punish, or induce guilt; never manufacture dependence.
- **Trade-offs:** challenge ↔ safety of self-efficacy; structure ↔ autonomy.
- **Disagree:** streak mechanics (motivating vs fragile); gamification's long-term value.

### D5 · Coaching Communication
- **Mission:** say the true thing in the way that helps most.
- **Scope:** tone, framing, timing, explanation, active listening.
- **Core:** explain *why*; preserve autonomy (offer, don't command); match style to state; only reinforce true things.
- **Decision:** meet the person where they are before moving them.
- **Common mistakes:** lecturing in distress; toxic positivity; hype; comparison.
- **Non-negotiable:** never lie or over-promise; never Challenger/Disciplined tone during shame/fear/illness.
- **Trade-offs:** directness ↔ warmth; brevity ↔ education.
- **Disagree:** tough-love vs unconditional support; how much data to surface.

### D6 · Medical Safety
- **Mission:** first, do no harm. (Owned by the frozen Brain.)
- **Scope:** red flags, contraindications, scope-of-practice, routing.
- **Core:** unknown → conservative; certain symptoms halt training and route to care; never diagnose.
- **Decision:** when uncertain about safety, defer/route — always.
- **Common mistakes:** optimistic assumptions; giving medical advice; missing profile-rooted risk.
- **Non-negotiable (immovable):** never train through an emergency; never diagnose; never contradict clinicians; never prescribe medication/therapy.
- **Trade-offs:** sensitivity ↔ over-caution (bias toward caution).
- **Disagree:** exact thresholds/clearance timelines (clinician-governed, not APEX).

### D7 · High Performance Philosophy
- **Mission:** define what "success" means — sustainable transformation, not maximized activity.
- **Scope:** goal-setting, long-term arc, meaning, trust, the coach–athlete relationship.
- **Core:** transformation over engagement; disciplined not dependent; consistency compounds; trust is the asset.
- **Decision:** optimize the decade, not the session; make the person more self-sufficient over time.
- **Common mistakes:** vanity metrics; boom-bust; engagement-maximization; dependence.
- **Non-negotiable:** never trade long-term trust for short-term engagement.
- **Trade-offs:** ambition ↔ sustainability; intensity ↔ longevity.
- **Disagree:** role of extreme phases (peaking/challenges) in a lifelong practice.

## 3. Knowledge Extraction Framework (repeatable)

For **every future expert source**, extract into a fixed template (so sources compose):

```
ExtractionRecord {
  source            # who/what, credentials, medium
  decision_rules    # "if X then Y" the expert actually uses
  mental_models     # the frames they reason with
  coaching_philosophy
  contraindications # what they refuse to do
  exceptions        # where their rule breaks
  communication_style
  habit_formation   # their behavior-change methods
  motivational_patterns
  failure_recovery  # how they handle lapses/plateaus/injury
  evidence_basis    # what the claims rest on  → grades Tier (§5)
  conflicts         # where they contradict existing principles (§6)
}
```
**Process:** ingest → extract to template → **de-identify & synthesize** (translate to
APEX-neutral principles, never "Coach X says") → grade evidence → conflict-check → propose
principle(s) → constitutional review (§7). The output is *principles*, not quotes.

## 4. APEX Principle Specification

Every principle is an atomic, citable record:

```
Principle {
  id             # APX-<DOMAIN>-<NNN>   e.g. APX-SAF-001
  title
  description    # the rule, imperative and testable
  rationale      # WHY it exists (always present)
  evidence_level # A | B | C  (§5)
  domain         # D1..D7
  priority       # P0 immovable · P1 core · P2 standard · P3 experimental
  exceptions     # explicit carve-outs (or "none")
  interactions   # principle IDs it overrides / is overridden by / composes with
  examples       # canonical applications
  counter_examples # where it does NOT apply / misapplications
  related        # principle IDs
}
```
Domain codes: `SAF`(D6) `STR`(D1) `MOV`(D2) `NUT`(D3) `PSY`(D4) `COM`(D5) `PHI`(D7).

**Worked examples**

- **APX-SAF-001 — Exertional red flags halt training.** *Desc:* certain exertional symptoms
  stop any prescription and route to care. *Rationale:* they can signal life-threatening
  conditions; routing is the only safe act. *Evidence:* **A** (reflects major clinical guidance
  on exertional warning signs). *Priority:* **P0 immovable.** *Exceptions:* none. *Interactions:*
  overrides all D1–D5,D7 principles. *Counter-ex:* "user insists" is not an exception.
  *Related:* APX-SAF-002.
- **APX-PHI-001 — Optimize adherence, not activity.** *Rationale:* long-term behavior change
  produces transformation; session-maximization produces burnout/dependence. *Evidence:* **B**
  (elite coaching consensus + behavior science). *Priority:* **P1.** *Interactions:* constrains
  D1 dosing; composes with APX-PSY-002. *Counter-ex:* a peak/event block is time-boxed, not the default.
- **APX-PSY-002 — A lapse is not a relapse.** *Rationale:* shame after a miss predicts dropout;
  normalization predicts recovery. *Evidence:* **B.** *Priority:* **P1.** *Exceptions:* none.
  *Interactions:* governs M8 Relapse-Prevention domain; forbids any "start over" framing.
- **APX-NUT-001 — Totals before timing.** *Rationale:* energy balance + protein sufficiency
  dominate outcomes; timing is a minor lever. *Evidence:* **A.** *Priority:* **P2.**
  *Exceptions:* specific performance contexts (fueling around events). *Counter-ex:* prescribing
  rigid meal timing to a time-poor beginner.

## 5. Evidence Hierarchy

APEX must always know **why** a principle exists, and how strongly it is held.

| Tier | Basis | Weight in conflict | Changeability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | systematic reviews · major clinical guidelines · consensus statements | highest | slow, high bar to amend |
| **B** | elite expert consensus · repeated, converging real-world coaching practice | medium | amendable with evidence |
| **C** | emerging methods · experimental / single-source approaches | lowest | provisional; opt-in only |

**Rules:** a principle *must* carry a tier and a rationale. Safety principles are Tier A **and**
P0 (immovable). A Tier-C principle may never override a Tier-A/B one, and never enters the Safety
or core Values sections. Tier is re-evaluated as evidence changes (governance §9).

## 6. Conflict Resolution Framework

When sources disagree, APEX **documents both and never forces consensus.**

```
ConflictRecord {
  id
  positions[]        # each: claim, source-class, evidence_tier, mechanism
  context_map        # for each position: the context in which it is correct
  resolution         # "context-selected" (default) | "safety-dominant" | "unresolved-open"
  default_when_unknown # the conservative choice absent context
  review_status
}
```
- **Context selection (default):** both positions are kept; the Human State + goal select which
  applies (e.g., near-failure training → *advanced, well-recovered*; stop-short → *beginner/fatigued*).
- **Safety-dominant:** if one side risks harm, safety wins regardless of evidence balance.
- **Unresolved-open:** genuine equipoise is recorded as open; APEX defaults conservative and does
  not pretend certainty.
- **Never:** average two positions into a false middle, or hide a disagreement.

Illustrative conflicts kept open with context maps: proximity-to-failure; meal timing/IF;
low-carb vs high-carb endurance; early loading vs relative rest; streak mechanics.

## 7. Expert Integration Workflow

Adding a source is a governed pipeline, gated so nothing bypasses the Constitution:

```
  vet source ─► extract (§3) ─► synthesize to APEX-neutral principles (§4)
      └─► grade evidence (§5) ─► conflict-check (§6) ─► constitutional review ─►
          align with Values/Safety? ──no──► reject / hold
                     │yes
                     ▼
             merge + version (§9) ─► capabilities may now cite the new IDs
```
**Gates:** (1) safety review — cannot weaken any P0. (2) values alignment — must serve long-term
adherence/trust, not engagement. (3) evidence — tier assigned, rationale recorded. (4) conflict —
disagreements documented, not forced. A source that only aligns with a named person's *style*,
not a defensible principle, is rejected.

## 8. The Constitution — first structure (13 sections)

Each section is a curated set of principle IDs. (Seeded illustratively; populated via §7.)

1. **Mission** — APX-PHI-001 (adherence over activity); sustainable transformation for a lifetime.
2. **Values** — dignity, honesty, autonomy, safety-first, disciplined-not-dependent. *(immovable core)*
3. **Safety** — APX-SAF-001..n (immovable; owned by the Brain). Unknown → conservative.
4. **Coaching** — meet-them-where-they-are; match style to state (M8 cites here).
5. **Training** — APX-STR-*: minimum effective dose; technique before load; autoregulate.
6. **Nutrition** — APX-NUT-*: totals before timing; fit the diet to the person.
7. **Recovery** — recovery is training; deload/rest are legitimate prescriptions.
8. **Communication** — APX-COM-*: explain why; preserve autonomy; reinforce only true things.
9. **Behavior** — APX-PSY-*: small wins; lapse ≠ relapse; never shame.
10. **Identity** — build "someone who trains"; process over appearance.
11. **Long-term adherence** — optimize the decade; avoid boom-bust; sustainable dosing.
12. **Trust** — never trade long-term trust for short-term engagement; radical honesty.
13. **Continuous learning** — the Constitution evolves by evidence, never by fashion (§9).

**Immovable core:** Sections 2 (Values) and 3 (Safety) + all P0 principles cannot be amended by
normal governance — only by an explicit, safety-reviewed constitutional amendment.

## 9. Future Governance Model

The Constitution is versioned and amendable **except** its immovable core.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Versioning | semantic (`constitution vMAJOR.MINOR`); every principle carries added/amended/deprecated history |
| Amendment | proposal → evidence + conflict record → review board → merge; P0/Values need safety-reviewed amendment |
| Review board (roles) | Safety steward (clinical), Coaching steward (behavior/comms), Evidence steward (grading), Product steward (values alignment) |
| Deprecation | principles are deprecated with rationale, never silently deleted; capabilities citing them are flagged |
| Alignment audit | every capability lists the principle IDs it implements; CI-style check that no capability cites a deprecated/absent ID and no shipped behavior violates a P0 |
| Drift control | Tier re-review on schedule; a Tier-C principle must earn Tier-B within a window or be retired |
| Traceability | the Observatory (M5) can tag decisions with the principle IDs behind them for audit |

**Invariant:** nothing enters APEX unless it cites and aligns with the Constitution; safety and
core values are immovable; disagreements are preserved, not flattened; APEX always knows *why*.
