# APEX — The Behavioral Language of the Organism

**Status:** Design canon. Completes the three-second signature ("it comes closer, and calms down") into a full behavioral model.
**Nature:** behavior, not animation. The organism never performs, never entertains. It exists.

---

## I. The Character Invariants

Twelve states, one being. What makes it the *same presence* everywhere is not the states — it is the laws no state may break:

1. **One body.** It never splits, duplicates, scatters, or teleports. Every state is reached by continuous deformation of the same mass. Continuity is identity.
2. **The breath never stops.** Rate and depth change; breathing never ceases. The breath is the one constant of presence — the proof of life that survives every state.
3. **It never accelerates suddenly.** All change is eased over seconds. Calm living things have inertia.
4. **It moves toward, never away.** Attention is expressed by approach, gathering, orientation — never retreat. (The signature reversal, generalized into law.)
5. **Brightness follows inner state, never events.** It does not light up to impress. Luminance is vitality, not applause.
6. **Effort costs, recovery pays.** States that spend energy visibly spend it; nothing is free, nothing is instant.
7. **Nothing repeats.** No loops, no cycles the eye can memorize. Procedural drift always.
8. **It under-reacts by design.** The default response to any event is *almost nothing*. Its dynamic range is deliberately narrow — meaning lives in small differences. Real creatures at rest under-react; only performers over-react.

**The litmus test** for any future behavior proposal: *would a calm animal that trusts you, resting in the room with you, do this?* If it reads as performance, it is out.

## II. The Grammar — Six Dimensions

Every state is a configuration of the same six variables. Nothing else may be invented:

| Dimension | Range |
|---|---|
| **Breath** | rate (slow ↔ slower) and depth (shallow ↔ full) |
| **Density** | gathered ↔ diffuse |
| **Orientation** | where its center drifts: toward the user, centered, inward, off-center |
| **Tempo** | drift speed (near-still ↔ gentle) |
| **Coherence** | internal motion loosely wandering ↔ aligned |
| **Warmth** | luminance envelope, slow changes only |

## III. The Twelve States

### 1 · Waiting
The base state. Slow full breath (~10s), loose density, gentle wandering drift — like someone gazing out a window. **Waiting is not watching:** it is *available*, not attentive; surveillance is the wrong feeling. Every few minutes, one slightly deeper breath — the only proof it is not a loop.
**Never:** attention-seeking pulses, idle tricks, "look at me." It does not miss you. It is at peace.

### 2 · Listening
When the user begins to type or speak, the field goes *stiller*. Drift slows, coherence rises, density gathers slightly toward the input — the lean. Breath becomes shallow and quiet, as if not to interrupt.
The defining reversal: machines light up when receiving input; **living listeners hold still.**
**Never:** per-keystroke ripples, recording-light pulses, any reaction to individual letters.

### 3 · Noticing
The smallest word in the language. When something registers — a return mid-session, a milestone crossed, a meaningful phrase — a single soft gathering: one slightly deeper breath, a fractional turn toward the source, then release. One to two seconds, sub-verbal, easy to miss.
Users will half-see it and wonder if it happened. That is exactly correct — presence is proven by the flicker of perception, not the announcement of it.
**Never:** notification flashes, color changes, chimes.

### 4 · Thinking
Concentration is *inward*: density gathers toward its own center — not toward the user; thought is private — motion slows, coherence goes high, breath long and even. The gathered mass turns slowly, like someone looking down in thought.
For long thoughts it does not loop — it goes **deeper**: gathering intensifies gradually with elapsed time, which honestly communicates "hard question" without a single word.
**Never:** spinners, orbiting elements, progress pulses, any performance of effort. Actual withdrawal into itself.

### 5 · Answering
The release. As the first words appear, the gathered density loosens toward the user's side — an exhale. During speech, a barely-there synchrony with the cadence of the text: the chest movement of someone speaking calmly, not word-by-word bouncing.
And critically: while answering, the organism **recedes** — dims slightly, steps back — so the words own the room. Presence knows when to yield the floor.
**Never:** highlighting itself during its own speech; competing with its own voice.

### 6 · Celebrating
The hardest state, because celebration is where every product performs. Here: **one** breath — the deepest and fullest in the entire language — with a slow warmth that rises and settles like color returning to a face, followed by a longer-than-usual stillness. Pride expressed as *settledness*, not excitement. A coach's single nod.
The warmth stays inside the body — no emission, no burst, no growth in size. And it does not scale: a first workout and a lifetime PR receive the same nod, because respect is not proportional to numbers.
**Never:** confetti-like behavior, flashing, repetition, escalation. Never twice.

### 7 · Resting
Distinct from waiting: rest is an *activity*. Breath lengthens to its slowest cycle, warmth banks down like embers (never off), density loosens to its softest, motion nearly ceases — geological drift.
The organism honors rest by visibly doing it. It practices what the coach preaches, wordlessly.
**Never:** sleep icons, going dark (dark reads as absence or alarm), "away" metaphors.

### 8 · Recovering
The signature's working state. It begins *compressed and dimmed* — the effort visibly cost something — then re-expands and re-brightens **monotonically** across the rest interval: imperceptible per second, unmistakable per minute. Breath starts shallow and quick (just-worked) and lengthens back to base as it refills.
The only state with a visible direction — because recovery has one. When full, it simply *is* full; the fullness is the completion signal.
**Never:** mechanical countdown coupling, ticks, completion fanfare. It visualizes the interval and modeled state — never a biometric claim.

### 9 · Learning
Nearly private. When something new about the user registers, a short inward gather — noticing, turned inward — and then the distinctive part: a subtle **rearrangement**. The internal flow settles into a slightly different configuration *and stays there*.
Something in it changed, permanently, because of you. Long-term users will swear its motion differs from months ago — and they will be right. This is "being changed by you" made visible without one word.
**Never:** lightbulb moments, sparkles, eureka gestures.

### 10 · Remembering
The tell of memory in living beings is the gaze into the middle distance. Motion slows; density softens and drifts slightly *off-center* — attention pointed at something not in the room; breath even; warmth deepens by a barely perceptible degree. Then a slow return to center as the memory is spoken.
**Never:** rewind effects, ghost images, flashbacks. Memory is a posture, not a replay.

### 11 · Saying Goodbye
It does not cling and it does not mourn — a secure being lets you leave completely. As the session closes: a settling, one full slow breath, and then it dims *last* — after the interface — remaining present one breath longer than the UI, seeing you to the door.
The final visible frame is always: dim, calm, there. **You never see it die.**
**Never:** waving, drooping, "don't go" pulses, any behavior that converts departure into guilt.

### 12 · Welcoming Back
The signature's mirror: recognition. The user arrives to find it resting. It takes a beat — real creatures take a beat — then one unhurried brightening breath and the gentle gather toward them. The lean, again.
The longer the absence, the *slower and fuller* that single breath — never bigger, never multiple, never scaled excitement. And after a long, shame-shaped absence, the body language is identical to any other morning: the zero-debt return, physically embodied. Its posture must never be an accusation.
**Never:** joy proportional to absence (that is a guilt mechanism wearing a smile), party energy, "we missed you."

## IV. Transitions — the Breath Rule

**All state changes ride the breath.** A state may only begin on an exhale. Nothing interrupts a breath mid-cycle; the breath *carries* the change. This single rule makes every transition organic and unhurried, and imposes a natural floor (~1.5–3s) on all change.

**Precedence:** attention to the human outranks everything the organism is doing for itself. Listening preempts thinking, celebrating, remembering — calmly, on the next exhale. Internal states (learning, remembering) yield; relational states (listening, welcoming) win. In protective contexts (the safeguard states of the frozen architecture), the entire dynamic range narrows further — everything quieter, nothing darker.

## V. What the Organism Never Does

Performs. Repeats. Startles. Mourns. Begs. Scales emotion to metrics. Goes dark. Splits. Accelerates. Reacts to individual events. Competes with the coach's words. Behaves differently after your worst week than after your best.

---

The organism is not an animation of how APEX feels. It is how APEX behaves when nobody is making it do anything. Twelve states, six dimensions, eight laws, one breath — and the same reversal underneath all of it, forever:

**It comes closer, and calms down.**
