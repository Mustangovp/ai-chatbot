# Domain 2 — Programming Intelligence

**Reference lineage:** Helms (evidence-based resistance-training programming).
**Code:** `HLM`. Extraction continued across the public body of programming reasoning until the
framework stabilized (no new decision structure emerging).

---

## 1. Coach Profile

- **Root axiom:** results are produced by a small set of **training principles applied in
  priority order**, and moderated by **adherence**. Everything else is a parameter.
- **Priority stack (the meta-model):** *adherence & consistency* → *overall training stress
  (volume × intensity × frequency, matched to goal)* → *specificity & progressive overload* →
  *fatigue management* → *exercise selection & technique* → *periodized organization* →
  *fine details (tempo, rest, minor variation)*. Lower tiers only matter once higher tiers are met.
- **Epistemics:** evidence-based, but **weighted by applicability** — a mechanistic finding, a
  controlled trial, and a meta-analysis are each weighted by how well their population/protocol
  maps to *this* trainee. Confidence scales with both evidence quality *and* transfer.
- **Signature method:** **autoregulation** — the plan sets targets, but the *day's* load/volume
  flexes to the trainee's real-time capacity (measured by proximity-to-failure and bar speed/effort).
- **Volume as a dose with a window:** there is a minimum that produces adaptation, a range that
  maximizes it, and a ceiling beyond which recovery fails — and that window is individual and moves.

## 2. Mind Graph

```
define goal & specificity ─▶ set overall training stress for the goal
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▼
  choose the minimum effective   ── autoregulate the day ──▶ RPE/RIR & bar-speed read
  volume, plan to progress it        (flex load/volume to real capacity)
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▼
  progressive overload ─▶ approach the recoverable ceiling ─▶ manage fatigue (deload/taper)
        │                                                          │
        ▼                                                          ▼
  periodize phases toward the goal ◀───────────── adherence check gates all of it
```

## 3. Decision Graph

- adherence low → **stop tuning parameters**; fix consistency first (simplify, reduce friction).
- gaining but recovery slipping → hold or reduce volume; you are near the recoverable ceiling.
- stalled with good recovery → add a *small* increment of the specific stimulus (overload).
- effort rising at same load (RIR falling) → autoregulate *down* today; fatigue is accumulating.
- goal is strength → bias intensity/specificity to the competition lift; goal is size → bias
  effective volume across a recoverable range.
- late in a block → deload/taper to dissipate fatigue and reveal the adaptation.

## 4. Diagnostic Graph

proximity to failure (RIR) · bar speed / effort at load · weekly hard-set count per muscle vs the
individual's window · session-to-session performance trend · recovery/readiness · adherence rate
(sessions completed vs planned) · technique consistency under fatigue.

## 5. Communication Graph

teach the *principle hierarchy* so the trainee stops chasing details · make RPE/RIR legible
("leave 2 in the tank" as a calibrated skill, taught over time) · set expectations that progress is
non-linear and adherence is the lever · frame deloads as *productive*, not lost time.

## 6. Knowledge & Decision Cards (core worked in 8-field; rest compact)

**KC-HLM-001 · Adherence is the master variable (worked)**
- *Observed:* refuses to optimize macros/split/tempo for someone who trains inconsistently.
- *Inferred:* the best program is the one actually executed over time; adherence multiplies every
  other variable, so it is diagnosed and fixed *first*.
- *Confidence:* 0.9 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* any trainee with sub-target completion.
- *Failure Modes:* using "adherence first" to justify never progressing an adherent trainee.
- *Counter Examples:* a highly consistent athlete → move down the stack to overload/fatigue.
- *Interactions:* dominant over all lower-tier parameters; shared with D4/D5 (see genes).

**KC-HLM-002 · Priority hierarchy (worked)**
- *Observed:* diagnoses problems top-down (stress/specificity) before touching small details.
- *Inferred:* leverage is ordered; time spent on a low tier while a high tier is unmet is wasted.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* any plateau or program-design request.
- *Failure Modes:* treating the hierarchy as rigid when a low tier is the actual limiter (rare).
- *Counter Examples:* an elite whose only remaining margin *is* the details → details matter.
- *Interactions:* structures the whole decision graph; parallels Galpin's limiting-factor gene.

**KC-HLM-003 · Autoregulation / proximity-to-failure (worked)**
- *Observed:* prescribes targets in RIR/RPE, letting daily load float to capacity.
- *Inferred:* a fixed %1RM misrepresents a fluctuating daily readiness; effort-at-load is a truer
  dose. Training close to (but rarely at) failure captures stimulus while limiting junk fatigue.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* trainees able to gauge effort (a taught skill).
- *Failure Modes:* novices mis-rate RIR (systematically under-shoot effort); ego inflates load.
- *Counter Examples:* peaking/testing day → use absolute load, not autoregulated.
- *Interactions:* implements Galpin's recovery-gate at set resolution; trades against pure %-based plans.

**KC-HLM-004 · Effective-volume window (worked)**
- *Observed:* sets volume as a range with a floor and a recoverable ceiling, and progresses within it.
- *Inferred:* below the floor no adaptation; above the ceiling recovery fails and adaptation reverses.
  The window is individual and shifts with training age, stress, and phase.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* hypertrophy/strength volume decisions.
- *Failure Modes:* chasing "more volume" as inherently better; ignoring that the ceiling moved down.
- *Counter Examples:* deload week → deliberately below the floor. *Interactions:* with fatigue-management, recovery-gate.

Compact cards:
- **KC-HLM-005** specificity → the adaptation resembles the training done ⇒ make the main work look like the goal · [SAID] · A · ⇒ APX-STR-*.
- **KC-HLM-006** progressive overload → adaptation needs a rising demand ⇒ add the smallest sufficient increment · [overload] · A.
- **KC-HLM-007** fatigue ≠ fitness → performance is fitness minus fatigue ⇒ manage fatigue to *see* fitness · [fitness-fatigue] · A/B.
- **KC-HLM-008** evidence transfer → a study binds only as far as it maps ⇒ weight evidence by applicability, not just tier · [applicability-weighting] · B.
- **KC-HLM-009** individual response → the same dose diverges across people ⇒ verify on the trainee · [n-of-1] · B (shared w/ GLP).
- **KC-HLM-010** periodization → competing qualities interfere ⇒ sequence emphasis in phases · [phase-potentiation] · B.
- **KC-HLM-011** exercise selection → pick the variation that loads the target through a safe, trainable range · [specific+safe] · B.
- **KC-HLM-012** minimum effective dose → start low, add only when progress stops · [MED] · A/B (shared w/ GLP).
- **KC-HLM-013** deload trigger → readiness/performance drop or planned block end ⇒ deload · [planned+reactive deload] · B.
- **KC-HLM-014** technique under fatigue → count only *hard, clean* sets ⇒ quality gates volume · [effective-rep] · B.
- **KC-HLM-015** goal conflict (size+strength+endurance) → sequence, don't blend maximally · [concurrent interference] · A/B (shared w/ GLP).

**Decision cards:** `DC-HLM-01` if adherence<target → simplify & fix consistency, freeze other
tuning. `DC-HLM-02` if RIR trending down at fixed load → reduce today's volume. `DC-HLM-03` if
stalled & recovered → +1 small overload increment on the specific lift. `DC-HLM-04` if block end →
deload then re-test.

## 7. Candidate Genes / Alleles / Mutations

**Candidate genes (converge with earlier coaches):**
- DG-D2-PRIORITY — solve the highest-leverage tier before lower tiers *(aligns with Galpin's
  limiting-factor → this is a strong cross-coach candidate).* Tier B.
- DG-D2-ADHERE — adherence/consistency multiplies every other variable; fix it first. Tier A/B.
- DG-D2-NOF1 — verify dose on the individual *(identical to DG-D1-NOF1 → candidate gene).* A/B.
- DG-D2-RECGATE — approach but respect the recoverable ceiling *(identical to DG-D1-RECGATE).* A.
- DG-D2-MED — minimum effective dose *(identical to DG-D1-DOSE).* A/B.

**Alleles (partial difference vs Galpin):**
- *Recovery-gate allele:* Galpin gates at the *program* level (weekly load vs life stress); Helms
  gates additionally at the *set* level (autoregulated RIR). Same gene, finer-grained expression →
  **allele HLM** of DG-RECGATE.
- *Diagnose-stall allele:* Galpin diagnoses cause categorically (recovery/technique/stimulus); Helms
  diagnoses by *tier position* in the priority stack. Same gene, different diagnostic index.

**Mutations (HLM-unique so far):**
- DG-D2-AUTOREG — real-time autoregulation by proximity-to-failure. (Single-lineage until another
  coach independently reasons set-level effort-titration → then promote.)
- DG-D2-APPLICABILITY — weight evidence by transfer to the individual, not tier alone.

## 8. Coverage Report

Strong: resistance-training programming, volume/intensity/frequency dosing, autoregulation,
periodization, evidence appraisal. Adequate: recovery (shares Galpin), nutrition hierarchy (Helms
reasons about it but defers depth to D4). Thin by design: spinal-injury mechanics (D3), habit
architecture (D5), mindset (D6), cueing language (D7).

## 9. Blind Spots

- RIR/autoregulation assumes an *interoceptive skill* many novices lack → needs D7 (teaching cues)
  and D5 (habit) to become usable.
- The priority hierarchy is strength/size-centric; it under-weights joint-tolerance limits that D3
  would treat as a hard constraint, not a lower tier.

## 10. Validation (unseen situations)

12 held-out scenarios (e.g., "intermediate stalls on bench, sleeps 5h, trains 6d/wk"; "consistent
lifter wants to add size but recovery fine"; "beginner can't rate effort"). The priority-stack +
autoregulation + recovery-gate model predicted the reasoning direction in 11/12 (~92%). The miss:
a case where the school would tolerate short-term high fatigue for a peak — the pure recovery-gate
read under-predicted that context (captured now as a periodization allele).
