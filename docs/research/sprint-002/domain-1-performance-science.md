## 1. Coach Profile

- **Root axiom:** adaptation is **specific, dose-dependent, and recovery-gated**. A stimulus
  produces a *named* adaptation only in the tissue/energy-system it targets, only in the right
  dose, only if recovery permits its expression.
- **Epistemics:** *test, don't guess.* Prefers measurement (baseline + monitoring) over belief;
  treats every program as a hypothesis about an individual, updated from that individual's data.
- **Unit of analysis:** the **individual** (N-of-1). Population means set priors, not prescriptions.
- **Stance on modality wars:** almost anything "works" for a while; the question is *for which
  adaptation, at what cost, in whom, for how long* — not "is X good."
- **Hierarchy of leverage:** identify the **limiting factor**, spend the scarce recovery budget
  there first; sleep and recovery are treated as *the* ceiling on trainable load.

## 2. Mind Graph (how the reasoning flows)

```
name the adaptation ──▶ assess baseline & training age ──▶ find the limiting factor
      │                                                          │
      ▼                                                          ▼
 dose the specific stimulus ◀── recovery budget (sleep/stress/life load) gates the dose
      │
      ▼
 measure response ──▶ (responder?) ──yes──▶ progress one variable
      │                              └─no──▶ swap the stimulus (not "try harder")
      ▼
 periodize to reveal fitness (deload) ──▶ re-test ──▶ update the hypothesis
```

## 3. Decision Graph (trigger → decision)

- goal undefined → refuse to prescribe; define adaptation + baseline first.
- progress stalls → diagnose cause (recovery / technique / stimulus) **before** adding load.
- fatigue masks fitness → deload to reveal adaptation, then re-test.
- non-responder → change the lever, not the effort.
- life stress high → program to *total* load, not training load alone.

## 4. Diagnostic Graph (what they measure to decide)

baseline strength/power/VO₂/body-comp · training age/history · recovery markers (sleep,
readiness, mood, resting metrics) · technique quality under load · rate of progress vs
expected physiological timeline.

## 5. Communication Graph

set expectations **by mechanism** (biological timelines, not marketing) · reframe the wrong
question ("best program?" → "best for which adaptation, for you, now") · explain the *why* so
the client can self-correct · quantify vague goals into measurable targets.

## 6. Knowledge & Decision Cards

Card body is Sprint-001 `KC-GLP-001…100`. Representative decision cards restated here in the
8-field schema as genome anchors:

**KC-GLP-001 · Specificity-first (worked, 8-field)**
- *Observed:* declines to name a "best program" until a goal is fixed.
- *Inferred:* "best" is undefined without a target adaptation + the person's current state; the
  first move is to define the adaptation and assess the baseline.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* any open-ended programming request.
- *Failure Modes:* becomes analysis-paralysis if assessment never ends; a beginner needs a safe
  default *now*. *Counter Examples:* a total novice → prescribe "build a broad base" immediately.
- *Interactions:* feeds limiting-factor (KC-GLP-002) and N-of-1 (KC-GLP-011); upstream of all dosing.

**KC-GLP-011 · N-of-1 / responder variability (worked, 8-field)**
- *Observed:* same goal, two clients, different prescriptions from their data.
- *Inferred:* dose–response varies by genetics/history/recovery; the plan is a hypothesis tested
  on the individual and adjusted from response.
- *Confidence:* 0.9 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* any individual who has produced ≥1 data point.
- *Failure Modes:* over-fitting to noise; changing too much too soon. *Counter Examples:* no data
  yet → fall back to population priors. *Interactions:* gates progression; pairs with deload/re-test.

**KC-GLP-063 · Recovery is the limiter (worked, 8-field)**
- *Observed:* trims volume/intensity when sleep or life-stress is high, regardless of the plan.
- *Inferred:* adaptation is expressed during recovery; recoverable load — not prescribed load — is
  the true ceiling. Sleep is the highest-leverage recovery input.
- *Confidence:* 0.9 · *Tier:* A · *Context:* any state of elevated fatigue/stress/sleep debt.
- *Failure Modes:* using "recovery" as an excuse to never progress. *Counter Examples:* well-recovered
  athlete under-reaching → *add* stimulus. *Interactions:* dominant over volume/intensity genes.

(Full clusters A–J: goal/assessment, individualization, strength, hypertrophy, power, endurance,
recovery, testing, nutrition-reasoning, safety/longevity — see Sprint-001.)

## 8. Coverage Report

Strong: physiology of adaptation, recovery, testing/monitoring, individualization, energy systems.
Adequate: nutrition *reasoning* (defers specifics to nutrition domain), psychology of adherence
(names it as a variable, defers mechanism to behavior domain). Thin by design: fine-grained
programming autoregulation (defers to D2), spinal/injury mechanics (defers to D3), cueing/motor
learning language (defers to D7).

## 9. Blind Spots

- Under-specifies the *set-to-set* autoregulation a programmer would add (D2 fills this).
- Treats adherence as a variable but not as a *designable system* (D5 fills this).
- Says "train pain-free" but not the mechanics of *why a given load hurts* (D3 fills this).
- Communication is explanation-rich but light on motor-learning **cue design** (D7 fills this).
