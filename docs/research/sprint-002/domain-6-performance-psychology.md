# Domain 6 — Performance Psychology

**Reference lineage:** Gervais (high-performance mindset & self-regulation).
**Code:** `GRV`. Psychological genes shape *delivery and self-regulation*; they never override
safety (D3), medical routing, or physiological limits (D1).

---

## 1. Coach Profile

- **Root axiom:** the mind is **trainable** like the body, and performance is gated by the ability to
  be **present and self-regulated under pressure**. Skill and fitness only express through a mind
  that isn't hijacked by fear or distraction.
- **Control the controllables:** attention goes to what is in the person's control — **effort,
  attitude, preparation, the next action** — not outcomes, comparisons, or others' opinions. Process
  focus is protective *and* performance-enhancing.
- **Relationship with fear:** the dominant hidden constraint is **fear** — of failure, of judgment,
  of not being enough. Progress comes from changing one's *relationship* to fear (acknowledge, allow,
  act) rather than suppressing or being ruled by it.
- **Presence & self-talk:** the practical levers are **attention training** (returning to the present
  moment), **breath** (down-regulating arousal), and **self-talk** (the internal narrative is a
  trainable input, not a fixed fact).
- **Purpose & identity:** durable drive comes from **values/purpose** and a chosen identity, which
  stabilize effort when motivation and results wobble.

## 2. Mind Graph

```
establish psychological safety (no shame) ─▶ name the real constraint (often fear/self-judgment)
        │
        ▼
 redirect attention to the controllable next action (process, not outcome)
        │
        ▼
 regulate arousal (breath) + regulate narrative (self-talk) ─▶ return to presence
        │
        ▼
 act from values/identity ─▶ review by effort & learning, not only result
        │
        ▼
 repeat as trainable practice (mindset is reps, not a switch)
```

## 3. Decision Graph

- outcome anxiety / comparison → redirect to the **controllable** next action; drop the scoreboard.
- fear/avoidance present → **name it, normalize it, act alongside it**; don't wait for fear to vanish.
- arousal too high (pressure) → **breath/down-regulation** before technique cues.
- harsh self-talk after a miss → treat the narrative as an input to retrain, not a truth.
- motivation dip → reconnect to **purpose/values** and identity, not to willpower.
- shame/psychological unsafety detected → restore safety first; nothing trains under threat.

## 4. Diagnostic Graph

where is attention (process vs outcome vs others)? · is fear/self-judgment the real limiter? ·
arousal level under pressure · quality/tone of self-talk · connection to purpose/values · presence
vs rumination (past/future) · psychological safety in the coaching relationship.

## 5. Communication Graph

create safety first (curiosity, no shame) so the truth surfaces · language of *choice and control*
("what's in your control right now?") · normalize fear rather than deny it · reinforce effort and
learning, not just outcome · teach breath and self-talk as trainable skills, patiently.

## 6. Knowledge & Decision Cards (core worked in 8-field; rest compact)

**KC-GRV-001 · Control the controllables / process focus (worked)**
- *Observed:* redirects attention from outcomes and comparison to the controllable next action.
- *Inferred:* attention on uncontrollables generates anxiety and degrades performance; process focus
  restores agency and frees working capacity for the task.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* pressure, plateaus, outcome fixation, comparison.
- *Failure Modes:* using "process only" to avoid honest outcome accountability entirely.
- *Counter Examples:* a competition where outcome data *must* inform strategy → outcome is relevant input.
- *Interactions:* converges with Clear's systems-over-goals and Galpin's process framing → gene.

**KC-GRV-002 · Relationship with fear (worked)**
- *Observed:* surfaces fear as the real constraint and coaches acting *with* it rather than removing it.
- *Inferred:* fear is not eliminable and suppression backfires; changing the relationship (acknowledge,
  allow, choose action) removes fear's veto over behavior.
- *Confidence:* 0.75 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* avoidance, performance anxiety, self-judgment.
- *Failure Modes:* pushing "act through fear" into ignoring a fear that signals real danger (D3 safety).
- *Counter Examples:* fear signaling genuine physical risk → heed it (safety dominates). *Interactions:* bounded by D3.

**KC-GRV-003 · Self-regulation before execution (worked)**
- *Observed:* down-regulates arousal (breath) and narrative (self-talk) before demanding technique.
- *Inferred:* an over-aroused, hijacked nervous system cannot execute fine motor skill or learn; the
  regulation must precede the instruction.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* high-pressure or high-arousal performance/learning.
- *Failure Modes:* endless regulation that never gets to the task. *Counter Examples:* low-arousal, calm state → go straight to execution.
- *Interactions:* sequences *before* D7's cueing (regulate, then cue) → cross-domain ordering gene.

**KC-GRV-004 · Psychological safety first (worked)**
- *Observed:* builds a no-shame, curious relationship before pushing performance or truth-telling.
- *Inferred:* under threat/shame people hide the real limiter and cannot learn; safety is the
  precondition for honest diagnosis and durable change.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* the whole coaching relationship, especially early.
- *Failure Modes:* mistaking safety for the absence of challenge (safety enables challenge, not avoids it).
- *Counter Examples:* an urgent safety/medical issue → act first, process feelings after. *Interactions:* enables D5 lapse-handling and honest D1–D4 diagnosis.

## 7. Candidate Genes / Alleles / Mutations

**Candidate genes:**
- DG-D6-PROCESS — direct attention/effort to the controllable process over the uncontrollable outcome
  *(converges with Clear systems-over-goals and Galpin process framing → strong gene).* Tier B.
- DG-D6-IDENTITY — anchor drive to purpose/values/identity *(identical structure to DG-D5-IDENTITY →
  reinforces the identity gene).* Tier B.
- DG-D6-SAFETY-PSYCH — establish psychological safety before pushing truth/performance *(new axis;
  watch for D7 convergence on autonomy-supportive language → likely gene).* Tier B.
- DG-D6-REGULATE-FIRST — regulate state before instruction/execution. Tier A/B.

**Alleles:**
- *Anchor allele:* the identity/why gene expressed as *values & purpose* (Gervais) vs *self-image
  votes* (Clear) vs *goal-value* (Galpin) — one gene, three context-selected alleles.
- *Review allele:* "judge by process/learning" (Gervais) vs "judge by measured response" (Galpin) —
  same accountability gene, subjective vs objective index.

**Mutations (GRV-unique so far):**
- DG-D6-FEAR — change the *relationship* with fear rather than eliminate it. (Psychology-specific;
  bounded hard by D3 — fear that signals real danger is heeded, not overridden.)
- DG-D6-PRESENCE — attention-to-present as a trainable performance lever.

## 9. Blind Spots

- Can under-weight the physical/technical cause of a "mental" block (sometimes the issue is a real
  D3 pain or D1 fatigue, not mindset) → must be diagnosed against the physical domains first.
- Powerful but slow; needs D5's concrete small-step scaffolding to convert insight into behavior.

## 10. Validation (unseen situations)

11 held-out scenarios (e.g., "freezes under competition pressure"; "quits when comparing to others";
"harsh self-talk after a bad session"; "avoids heavy lifts out of fear"). The process-focus +
regulate-first + fear-relationship + safety model predicted the reasoning direction in 10/11 (~91%).
The miss: a case where the avoidance was actually a rational response to a real injury risk — the
psychological read initially over-rode what was correctly a D3 safety signal (now explicitly bounded).
