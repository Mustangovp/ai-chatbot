# Domain 5 — Behavior Change

**Reference lineage:** Clear (habit formation & behavioral design).
**Code:** `CLR`. The underlying mechanisms are public behavioral science (cue–response–reinforcement,
self-perception/identity, friction, environment design); reconstructed here in APEX-neutral terms
without any branded framework wording.

---

## 1. Coach Profile

- **Root axiom:** long-term outcomes are produced by **systems and identity**, not by goals or
  willpower. You do not rise to your goals; you default to your systems and your self-image.
- **Identity engine:** durable behavior change runs through **who the person believes they are**.
  Each action is a small vote for an identity ("someone who trains"); the aim is to make the behavior
  a proof of identity, not a chore toward a distant number.
- **The behavior loop:** a behavior is triggered by a **cue**, driven by an **anticipated reward**,
  performed as a **response**, and reinforced by the **felt reward**. To build a habit, engineer each
  stage; to break one, invert them.
- **Design over discipline:** make the wanted behavior **obvious, easy, and immediately rewarding**,
  and the unwanted behavior the opposite. **Reduce friction** for good behaviors; add friction to bad
  ones. The **environment** usually beats motivation.
- **Small and repeatable:** start with an action so small it cannot be refused; **consistency of a
  tiny action** compounds far past a heroic effort done once. Never miss twice — a lapse is data, not
  a verdict.

## 2. Mind Graph

```
anchor to identity ("become the kind of person who…") 
        │
        ▼
 shrink the action to the smallest repeatable version (cannot fail)
        │
        ▼
 make the cue obvious (time/place/after-an-existing-habit)
        │
        ▼
 reduce friction to start ─▶ perform ─▶ attach an immediate, felt reward
        │
        ▼
 track the streak; if missed, never miss twice ─▶ let repetition compound
```

## 3. Decision Graph

- motivation low / plan ambitious → **shrink the ask** to a 2-minute, cannot-fail version.
- behavior keeps not happening → the **cue** is weak or missing; attach it to an existing routine/place.
- relies on willpower → redesign the **environment** so the good choice is the default/easy one.
- client "failed" once → reframe as a lapse; the rule is *never miss twice*, restart immediately.
- distant goal not motivating → re-anchor to **identity** and to an **immediate** reward, not the far outcome.
- habit fragile → increase reward *salience* now, and make skipping it more effortful.

## 4. Diagnostic Graph

is there a clear cue (when/where/after-what)? · how small is the smallest version? · how much friction
to start? · is there an *immediate* reward or only a delayed one? · what identity does the person
claim? · streak/consistency data · what in the environment makes the wrong choice easy?

## 5. Communication Graph

speak to identity ("you're becoming someone who…") · celebrate showing up (reinforce the vote) ·
make the next step laughably small and specific · normalize lapses and remove shame (shame breaks
streaks) · design the ask so success is nearly guaranteed, then build.

## 6. Knowledge & Decision Cards (core worked in 8-field; rest compact)

**KC-CLR-001 · Identity-based change (worked)**
- *Observed:* frames behaviors as evidence of an identity rather than steps to a number.
- *Inferred:* self-image regulates behavior; when the behavior confirms "who I am," it needs less
  external motivation and survives setbacks.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* motivation, long-term adherence, relapse recovery.
- *Failure Modes:* identity talk without any easy action attached → no behavior to vote with.
- *Counter Examples:* acute, one-off task with no identity stake → just reduce friction and do it.
- *Interactions:* completes Galpin's "anchor to their why"; upstream of the reinforcement gene.

**KC-CLR-002 · Smallest repeatable action (worked)**
- *Observed:* reduces an ambitious plan to a trivially small starting version.
- *Inferred:* the hardest part is starting and *repeating*; a cannot-fail action secures consistency,
  and consistency is what compounds — magnitude can grow later.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* low motivation, new habits, overwhelmed clients.
- *Failure Modes:* staying tiny forever with no progression once the habit is stable.
- *Counter Examples:* an already-consistent, capable trainee → the limiter is dose, not starting.
- *Interactions:* directly serves Helms/Aragon adherence genes; pairs with cue design.

**KC-CLR-003 · Environment/friction over willpower (worked)**
- *Observed:* redesigns context so the good choice is the easy default rather than relying on resolve.
- *Inferred:* willpower is unreliable and depletes; changing friction and cues changes behavior more
  durably than exhortation.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* any repeated choice with environmental leverage.
- *Failure Modes:* over-engineering environment for behaviors that need skill, not ease.
- *Counter Examples:* a behavior blocked by capability/knowledge, not friction → teach, don't just ease.
- *Interactions:* converges with Aragon's satiety-design and budget-not-ban (friction on food) → notes gene.

**KC-CLR-004 · Never miss twice / lapse ≠ relapse (worked)**
- *Observed:* treats a single miss as neutral data and prioritizes immediate restart.
- *Inferred:* the damage from a lapse is mostly the *narrative* ("I've failed") that triggers a
  second and third miss; protecting the streak psychology preserves the habit.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* adherence dips, guilt-driven dropout.
- *Failure Modes:* using "lapse is fine" to normalize chronic missing. *Counter Examples:* a pattern of repeated misses → escalate to redesign, not just reassurance.
- *Interactions:* this is the mechanism behind APEX's existing "a lapse is not a relapse" coaching rule.

Compact cards:
- **KC-CLR-005** weak cue → anchor the behavior to an existing routine/time/place · [cue-anchoring] · B.
- **KC-CLR-006** delayed reward only → add an immediate, felt reward to reinforce now · [immediate-reinforcement] · B.
- **KC-CLR-007** systems > goals → design the repeatable process, not just the target · [systems-over-goals] · B.
- **KC-CLR-008** compounding → small consistent gains outrun sporadic big efforts · [aggregation] · B.
- **KC-CLR-009** tracking → make progress visible to reinforce the streak · [make-it-visible] · B.
- **KC-CLR-010** temptation pairing → attach a wanted-but-hard behavior to an enjoyed one · [bundling] · C/B.
- **KC-CLR-011** unwanted habit → add friction, hide the cue, delay the reward · [invert-the-loop] · B.
- **KC-CLR-012** plateau of latent potential → results lag effort; keep going through the flat part · [latency] · B.

**Decision cards:** `DC-CLR-01` if motivation low → shrink to a 2-minute action. `DC-CLR-02` if
behavior not happening → attach an obvious cue to an existing routine. `DC-CLR-03` if a miss occurs
→ restart next occurrence, never miss twice. `DC-CLR-04` if willpower-reliant → redesign environment.

## 7. Candidate Genes / Alleles / Mutations

**Candidate genes:**
- DG-D5-ADHERE-MECH — *how* to produce the adherence the other domains depend on: shrink, cue,
  reduce friction, reinforce immediately. This is the **mechanism** under Helms's & Aragon's
  adherence gene → they call *that* it matters; Clear calls *how*. Strong convergence → gene
  (with Clear supplying the sub-structure). Tier B.
- DG-D5-IDENTITY — anchor durable behavior to identity/intrinsic self-image *(completes Galpin's
  "anchor to their why" → cross-coach candidate).* Tier B.
- DG-D5-SMALLSTEP — reduce the ask to the smallest repeatable version to secure consistency
  *(aligns with minimum-effective-dose reasoning, applied to behavior).* Tier B.
- DG-D5-LAPSE — a lapse is data, not a verdict; protect the restart. Tier B.

**Alleles:**
- *Minimum-dose allele:* Galpin/Helms's "minimum effective dose" (physiological) and Clear's "smallest
  repeatable action" (behavioral) are the same gene expressed on different axes — dose-of-stimulus vs
  dose-of-friction. Context selects the allele.
- *Anchor allele:* "anchor to why" expressed as *identity* (Clear) vs *goal-value* (Galpin).

**Mutations (CLR-unique so far):**
- DG-D5-ENVDESIGN — change friction/environment rather than exhort. (Behavioral; partial overlap with
  Aragon's satiety/budget design — candidate merge into a broader "design the default" gene on review.)
- DG-D5-IMMEDIATE — attach immediate reinforcement to a delayed-reward behavior.

## 8. Coverage Report

Strong: habit formation, motivation architecture, relapse handling, environment design, identity.
Adequate: psychology of focus/pressure (defers to D6). Thin by design: all physiological/technical
domains (D1–D4, D7) — Clear supplies the *behavioral substrate* they each assume but don't build.

## 9. Blind Spots

- Silent on *what* to train/eat (correctly — it's a behavior domain); depends entirely on D1–D4 for content.
- Habit design assumes a baseline of psychological safety/regulation that D6 provides for high-pressure
  or fear-laden contexts.

## 10. Validation (unseen situations)

12 held-out scenarios (e.g., "keeps planning to train but never does"; "felt guilty after missing a
week and quit"; "relies on motivation that fades"; "wants a huge overhaul on day one"). The
identity + small-step + cue/friction + never-miss-twice model predicted the reasoning direction in
11/12 (~92%). The miss: a case requiring hard *capability* building (a skill deficit) where the
school's ease-first instinct under-served the need to actually teach — a reminder that friction
design ≠ skill acquisition.
