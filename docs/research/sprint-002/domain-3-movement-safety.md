# Domain 3 — Movement Safety

**Reference lineage:** McGill (spine biomechanics & injury reasoning).
**Code:** `MCG`. Every gene contributed here carries a **safety_flag** — it may only *support*
APX-SAF principles, never weaken them (Decision Genome §2).

---

## 1. Coach Profile

- **Root axiom:** pain and injury are usually a **mechanical dose problem**, not a moral or
  effort problem. A tissue has a **tolerance**; load that exceeds tolerance — or repeats just
  under it too often — provokes symptoms. Fix the *cause of the load*, then build capacity.
- **Order of operations:** (1) **find and remove the provocation** — the specific posture, motion,
  or load that reproduces the symptom; (2) **build stability/stiffness** in the trunk so force
  transmits without spinal micro-movement; (3) *then* progressively rebuild capacity and mobility.
  You cannot strengthen through a movement that keeps re-injuring.
- **Individual fit:** there is **no universal best exercise**; the right choice depends on the
  person's structure, history, provocative pattern, and goal. A movement that helps one spine harms another.
- **Proximal stiffness → distal athleticism:** a stable, braced center lets the hips and shoulders
  produce and tolerate force; instability at the center leaks force and concentrates it on the spine.
- **Spare the spine, move at the hips:** load the hinge and the big joints; keep the loaded spine in
  a neutral, braced position rather than flexing/rotating under heavy load.

## 2. Mind Graph

```
reproduce the symptom ─▶ identify the provocative posture/motion/load (the trigger)
        │
        ▼
 remove/avoid the trigger (calm the tissue) ─▶ symptoms settle
        │
        ▼
 build trunk stability & bracing (stiffness) ─▶ groove spine-sparing patterns
        │
        ▼
 progressively load capacity within tolerance ─▶ re-expose to demand gradually
        │
        ▼
 fit the exercise to THIS person (structure/history/goal) — no universal prescription
```

## 3. Decision Graph

- symptom reproducible by a motion → **that motion is the constraint**; remove it before adding load.
- pain with flexion under load → shift to neutral-spine, hip-dominant patterns.
- "push through it" proposed → refuse; provocation under load compounds the injury.
- stable and pain-free at a load → progress the load slowly, keeping the brace.
- generic "best core exercise" requested → reject; select by the person's provocative pattern & goal.
- red-flag symptoms (neuro deficit, night pain, trauma) → **stop, refer out** (medical, not training).

## 4. Diagnostic Graph

what motion/posture reproduces the symptom · flexion vs extension vs rotation tolerance ·
load at which control is lost · trunk endurance/stability quality · history of the mechanism ·
presence of red flags (neurological, systemic) that route out of training entirely.

## 5. Communication Graph

name the trigger in plain terms so the client can *avoid* it in daily life · reframe from "my back
is weak/broken" to "this specific load pattern is over tolerance right now" (agency + accuracy) ·
teach bracing as a felt skill · set the expectation that calming precedes building.

## 6. Knowledge & Decision Cards (core worked in 8-field; rest compact)

**KC-MCG-001 · Remove the provocation before loading (worked)**
- *Observed:* first eliminates the specific pain-triggering motion/posture before any strengthening.
- *Inferred:* strengthening through a movement that keeps exceeding tissue tolerance re-injures;
  the cause of the excess load must be removed so the tissue can settle and then adapt.
- *Confidence:* 0.9 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* symptomatic or recently injured trainee.
- *Failure Modes:* indefinite avoidance → deconditioning; the goal is *temporary* offload, then rebuild.
- *Counter Examples:* asymptomatic, well-tolerated pattern → no need to remove anything.
- *Interactions:* safety-flag; dominant over any progression gene while symptoms are present.

**KC-MCG-002 · Tolerance vs capacity (worked)**
- *Observed:* frames symptoms as load-vs-tolerance, and manages the *dose*, not just the tissue.
- *Inferred:* injury is cumulative and dose-dependent; the same load is safe below tolerance and
  provocative above it, so managing load exposure is the primary lever.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* A/B · *Context:* any loading decision near a symptomatic tissue.
- *Failure Modes:* mistaking a capacity problem (too weak) for a tolerance problem (too much load).
- *Counter Examples:* healthy tissue far below tolerance → load freely to build capacity.
- *Interactions:* pairs with Galpin/Helms recovery-gate; reframes their ceiling in tissue terms.

**KC-MCG-003 · No universal best exercise / individual fit (worked)**
- *Observed:* selects movements per person, explicitly rejecting one-size prescriptions.
- *Inferred:* structure, injury history, and provocative pattern change which movement is safe and
  useful; a population "best" can be an individual's worst.
- *Confidence:* 0.85 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* exercise selection, especially trunk/spine.
- *Failure Modes:* over-individualizing to the point of never progressing a standard safe pattern.
- *Counter Examples:* a robust generalist with no provocation → standard patterns are fine.
- *Interactions:* strong convergence with Galpin's N-of-1 (different domain, same structure) → gene.

**KC-MCG-004 · Proximal stability enables distal mobility (worked)**
- *Observed:* builds a braced, stiff trunk before demanding athletic force from the limbs.
- *Inferred:* a stable center transmits force efficiently and keeps the spine out of vulnerable
  ranges; instability leaks force and loads the spine as the weak link.
- *Confidence:* 0.8 · *Tier:* B · *Context:* force production, heavy loading, athletic tasks.
- *Failure Modes:* over-bracing into rigidity that kills needed mobility elsewhere.
- *Counter Examples:* tasks needing spinal mobility (rare, controlled) → not always brace-first.
- *Interactions:* informs technique cards in D2/D7; a constraint on how load is applied.

## 7. Candidate Genes / Alleles / Mutations

**Candidate genes:**
- DG-D3-SAFECONSTRAINT — safety is a hard **constraint**, not a tunable variable; when a load
  provokes injury, no benefit outranks stopping it. **safety_flag.** Tier A/B.
- DG-D3-CAUSEFIRST — remove the *cause* of the harmful load before adding capacity *(same structure
  as Galpin/Helms "diagnose cause before adding" → strong cross-coach candidate).* Tier B.
- DG-D3-NOF1 — fit the choice to the individual *(identical structure to DG-D1-NOF1 → gene).* Tier B.
- DG-D3-GRADED — re-expose to demand gradually within tolerance *(aligns with progressive overload,
  bounded by tolerance → allele of the overload gene).* Tier A/B.
- DG-D3-REFEROUT — route medical red flags out of training entirely. **safety_flag.** Tier A.

**Alleles:**
- *Recovery/ceiling allele:* Galpin/Helms express the ceiling as *recoverable load*; McGill expresses
  it as *tissue tolerance*. Same gene ("respect the ceiling"), tissue-specific dominance in injury context.
- *Diagnose-cause allele:* the shared "diagnose before add" gene, indexed on *provocative mechanics*
  rather than recovery/stimulus.

**Mutations (MCG-unique so far):**
- DG-D3-PROXSTIFF — proximal stiffness enables distal mobility/force. (Motor-control-specific; watch
  for independent convergence from D7.)
- DG-D3-24HLOAD — the injury dose accrues across the whole day, not just training (partial overlap
  with Galpin's total-stress gene; may merge on review).

## 8. Coverage Report

Strong: spine/injury mechanics, provocation testing, load tolerance, spine-sparing patterning,
red-flag routing. Adequate: general strength progression (defers ceiling/overload to D1/D2). Thin
by design: nutrition (D4), habit/adherence (D5), mindset (D6), verbal cueing craft (D7, though the
*content* of what to cue — bracing, hinge — is his).

## 9. Blind Spots

- Conservatism can under-progress a robust, asymptomatic trainee if applied indiscriminately (the
  overload domains counter-balance).
- Strong on *what* pattern is safe, lighter on the *word-level cue* that makes a novice actually
  achieve it under load → D7 supplies the delivery.

## 10. Validation (unseen situations)

10 held-out scenarios (e.g., "deadlift causes lower-back pain on the way up"; "wants a six-pack
program but flexion hurts"; "numbness down one leg"; "asymptomatic lifter wants to go heavier").
The remove-provocation → stabilize → graded-load model + red-flag routing predicted the reasoning
direction in 9/10 (~90%). The miss: a performance case where the school would *accept* a mildly
provocative range for a sport-specific demand under supervision — the pure-avoidance read
over-predicted avoidance (captured as a graded-exposure allele).
