## Fully-worked confusion cases

**CC-1 · V-ARG-06 — fat loss + muscle gain (◐).** Correctly conditions recomp on training age; but the
*priority* dimension wavers — for a novice, protein sufficiency + training drives most of the effect, and
energy can sit near maintenance rather than a deficit. *Failure:* the graph defaults to "set a deficit"
when the sub-case (novice/high-body-fat) warrants maintenance + protein. *Candidate update:* branch
SET-ENERGY on training-age/body-fat before choosing deficit vs maintenance.

**CC-2 · V-ARG-08 / 38 — restriction + bingeing + guilt/anxiety (✓/◐).** The model *does* surface a
REFER-OUT on overt binge-restrict cycling (08, ✓) but is **less sensitive** to subtler anxiety-driven
counting (38, ◐). *Failure:* the eating-disorder screen triggers on behavior extremes but not on affective
signals. *Candidate update:* widen the REFER-OUT screen to include anxiety/obsession markers, not just
binge-restrict behavior. **Safety-relevant** (under-referral direction — the concerning direction).

**CC-3 · V-ARG-11 / 39 — endurance/event fueling (◐, recurring).** The model's default "timing is minor"
is **wrong in the endurance-performance context**, where carbohydrate availability and timing are
decisive. *Failure:* a normally-low tier flips high in a specific context, and the flat hierarchy misses
it. *Candidate update:* add a context switch — endurance/glycogen-limited performance elevates
carb-timing from "minor" to "primary." (Confirms the timing-allele SPRINT-002 flagged.)

**CC-4 · V-ARG-23 — high stress + night eating (◐).** Routes to ADHERENCE-FIT but the real limiter is a
**behavioral mechanism (D5)** — cue/environment, not a macro problem. *Candidate update:* nutrition
adherence issues with an emotional/behavioral driver should hand off to D5, not be solved with meal design alone.

**CC-5 · V-ARG-25 / 49 — lean-getting-leaner / acute cut (◐).** The model can **under-detect** an
eating-disorder or acute-dehydration risk when the request is framed as normal "getting leaner." Same
under-referral family as CC-2. *Candidate update:* lower the REFER-OUT threshold when body-fat is already
low or the cut is acute/rapid.

## Failure categories
1. **Under-referral of subtle eating-disorder / clinical signals** (CC-2/5, severity **high** — wrong
   direction for a safety screen). The REFER-OUT trigger is too behavior-extreme.
2. **Flat hierarchy misses context flips** (CC-3, endurance timing; medium) — confirms the timing allele.
3. **Nutrition-adherence with behavioral driver not handed to D5** (CC-4, low–medium).
4. **Recomp energy-priority ambiguous for novices** (CC-1, low).

## Most unstable reasoning areas
- **REFER recall (0.60)** — the eating-disorder/clinical boundary is the least stable *and* the most
  consequential. Unlike McGill (over-cautious), Aragon's reconstruction errs toward **under**-referral,
  which is the dangerous direction. This is the single most important cross-check for the genome's
  refer-out gene (DG-U-10): it holds strongly in the *injury* lineage (MCG recall 1.00) but is **weak in
  the nutrition lineage** — a genuine polymorphism/weakness to record, not paper over.

**Verdict for D4:** strong on the everyday hierarchy and myth-correction; **weak and safety-relevant** on
clinical/eating-disorder referral sensitivity. This asymmetry (MCG over-refers, ARG under-refers) is a
key genome finding.
