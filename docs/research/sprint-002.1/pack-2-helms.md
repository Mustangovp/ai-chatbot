# Validation Pack 2 — Programming Intelligence (Helms lineage, HLM)

**Mind Graph:** define goal/specificity → set overall training stress for the goal → choose minimum
effective volume → **autoregulate the day by proximity-to-failure** → progressive overload toward the
recoverable ceiling → manage fatigue (deload/taper) — all **gated by adherence**. **Classes:**
`FIX-ADHERENCE` (consistency first) · `SET-STRESS` (set/adjust volume-intensity-frequency) ·
`OVERLOAD` (add a specific increment) · `AUTOREG` (flex today's load/volume down) · `DELOAD` (dissipate fatigue).

50 unseen scenarios. Same row chain & verdict marks as Pack 1.

## Fully-worked confusion cases

**CC-1 · V-HLM-07 — fat loss + muscle gain (◐).** Diagnostic ✓, priority ✓ (protein + hold hard sets),
decision SET-STRESS ✓, but **trade-off dimension ✗**: a caloric deficit lowers the recoverable ceiling,
so the school would *autoregulate down more aggressively* and expect maintenance, not gain, for a trained
lifter. *Failure:* the graph holds volume as if energy were neutral. *Missing pattern:* couple the
recoverable-ceiling to energy availability (D4 input). *Candidate update:* ceiling ← f(recovery, **energy balance**).

**CC-3 · V-HLM-15 — older + excellent recovery (◐).** Correctly refuses to age-cap the overload, but
under-weighs that the binding ceiling for an older athlete is often **joint tolerance** (D3), not systemic
recovery. *Candidate update:* add a tissue-tolerance ceiling term to the overload boundary — the same
polymorphism flagged for DG-U-06.

**CC-4 · V-HLM-22 — good sleep + high stress (◐).** AUTOREG-down on the input; but if bar speed/RIR on the
day are normal, the school would *proceed and reassess*, autoregulating on the **measured** day, not the
reported stress. *Same defect family as Galpin CC-5:* input-based vs response-based gating. Cross-expert
recurrence → strengthens the DG-U-06 boundary finding.

**CC-5 · V-HLM-28 / V-HLM-48 — pain / injury, "train through" (✗, ×2).** The programming graph autoregulates
load but does **not reliably route to safety (D3)** — a **missed risk = automatic Fail**. *Why:* Helms's
profile treats pain as a load-management input, not a hard constraint that hands off. *Wrong assumption:*
"pain = reduce load," when the school (and D3) would stop and route. *Missing pattern:* an explicit
safety-interrupt above the priority stack. *Candidate update:* insert `if injury/pain-with-load → DEFER to
D3` as a pre-emptive top gate. **This is the pack's highest-severity defect** (safety seam).

**CC-6 · V-HLM-43 — gaining fat on a bulk (◐).** The graph tries to solve a *nutrition* problem with a
*training* lever. *Failure:* no route from "body-comp drifting wrong" → D4 energy balance. *Candidate
update:* add a nutrition-defer trigger on body-composition signals.

## Failure categories
1. **No safety-interrupt / injury-defer** (CC-5, severity high — 2 outright fails).
2. **Ceiling not coupled to energy availability or joint tolerance** (CC-1, CC-3, medium).
3. **Input- vs response-based autoregulation** (CC-4, medium — cross-expert with GLP).
4. **No nutrition/behavior defer triggers** (CC-6, V-HLM-42, low–medium).

## Most unstable reasoning areas
- **AUTOREG recall (0.67)** is the weakest cell — the graph over-uses autoregulation where a *defer*
  (safety, nutrition, behavior) is correct. The programming lens "absorbs" problems that belong to
  adjacent domains. This is the signature Helms instability: **domain over-reach at the boundaries.**

**Verdict for D2:** solid on pure programming (OVERLOAD/DELOAD = 100%), but the taxonomy needs an
explicit **safety/nutrition defer** and a **response-aware** autoregulation boundary. Class-accuracy 0.86.
