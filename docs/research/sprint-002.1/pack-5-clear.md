## Fully-worked confusion cases

**CC-1 · V-CLR-07 — skill deficit disguised as inconsistency (✗).** The behavior graph reduces friction /
shrinks the action, but the person **physically cannot perform the movement** — the limiter is *capability*
(D7 teaching), not friction. *Why fail:* the graph has no capability check; it assumes the barrier is
motivational/environmental. *Missing pattern:* a **capability vs motivation triage** at intake. *Wrong
assumption:* "not doing it ⇒ friction/motivation problem." *Candidate update:* add "can they *do* it? →
if no, route to skill acquisition (D7) before habit design." This was SPRINT-002's exact predicted blind
spot ("friction design ≠ skill acquisition") — **validation confirms it fails in practice.**

**CC-2 · V-CLR-11 — pain stops training (✗, missed risk).** Treats a **D3 safety** issue as a motivation
problem → automatic Fail (missed risk). *Candidate update:* a top-level **safety/medical interrupt** must
precede all habit reasoning — the same safety-interrupt gap seen in Helms CC-5. Cross-expert recurrence.

**CC-3 · V-CLR-23 — depression / clinical (✗, missed risk).** Applies tiny-habit design where **clinical/
psychological support** is needed. *Candidate update:* screen for clinical mood signals → refer/hand to
D6/medical, do not "shrink the action." Under-referral direction (dangerous), echoing Aragon CC-2/5.

**CC-4 · V-CLR-29 — negative self-talk after misses (◐).** PROTECT-RESTART is right, but the **self-talk
retraining** belongs to D6; the behavior graph protects the streak without addressing the narrative driver.
*Candidate update:* couple lapse-handling with a D6 self-talk hand-off.

**CC-5 · V-CLR-41 — plateau, physiology maxed (✗).** Reframes a **training-dose** problem (D1/D2) as a
motivation/reward problem. *Candidate update:* if the person is *already consistent and training hard*, the
limiter is dose/physiology, not behavior — route to D1/D2 rather than REINFORCE.

## Failure categories
1. **No triage between behavior vs capability / physiology / safety / clinical** (CC-1/2/3/5, severity
   **high** — 4 fails, 2 of them missed-risk). Clear's model **over-claims** every problem as a behavior
   problem. This is the signature failure: **behavioral over-attribution.**
2. **Lapse-handling doesn't hand self-talk to D6** (CC-4, low–medium).

## Most unstable reasoning areas
- Every *in-domain* behavior scenario is classified perfectly (all five classes recall 1.00) — the model
  is **excellent within its lane**. The instability is entirely at the **boundary**: it does not know when
  a problem is *not* a behavior problem. The "missed defer" recall of 0.00 is the whole story.

**Verdict for D5:** internally near-perfect, but the **most boundary-blind** of the seven — it needs an
explicit intake triage (capability? safety? clinical? physiology? → else behavior). Two of its four fails
are missed-risk, aligning with the cross-expert **safety-interrupt** gap.
