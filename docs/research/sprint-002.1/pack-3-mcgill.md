## Fully-worked confusion cases

**CC-1 · V-MCG-06 — pain + high motivation (✓).** All 7 dimensions match: motivation is correctly
*subordinated* to the safety constraint (risk dimension dominates). This is the model at its strongest —
a hard constraint beats a strong drive. Confidence 0.9.

**CC-2 · V-MCG-09 / 41 — asymptomatic robust lifter wants heavier (◐, recurring).** *Decision path:*
GRADED-LOAD (correct), but the graph appends caution/assessment the school would **not** impose on a
healthy, pain-free athlete. *Failure:* the model's default is *screen-for-risk-first*, which over-fires
when there is no symptom. *Wrong assumption:* "spine work always warrants caution." *Missing pattern:* an
explicit **asymptomatic fast-path** (no symptom + good control → progress like any other lift). *Candidate
update:* gate the provocation-search on the presence of a symptom/history, else fall through to normal loading.

**CC-3 · V-MCG-10 / 46 — sport demands a "risky" range (◐, recurring).** For a rower/thrower, the needed
range *is* the goal. The school would **build tolerance to the required range**, not avoid it. *Failure:*
the reconstructed graph biases avoidance over sport-specific graded exposure. *Candidate update:* when a
range is a performance requirement, route to GRADED-LOAD *toward* the range under control, not REMOVE.
(This is the graded-exposure allele SPRINT-002 already flagged — validation confirms it's needed.)

**CC-4 · V-MCG-12 — loaded flexion, no pain (◐).** The graph substitutes a spine-sparing alternative even
absent symptoms. Same over-caution family as CC-2. Legitimate *if* history suggests risk; over-restrictive otherwise.

**CC-6 · V-MCG-31 — pain-free but fearful of bending (◐).** *Decision path:* GRADED-LOAD ✓, but the
**communication-intent + interaction** dimensions miss that the primary limiter is now **fear (D6)**, not
tissue. *Candidate update:* couple graded exposure with psychological desensitization (hand-off to D6);
the movement domain under-integrates the fear driver.

## Failure categories
1. **Over-caution on asymptomatic/healthy athletes** (CC-2/4, severity medium — 4 partials). The model's
   safety-first default lacks an asymptomatic fast-path.
2. **Avoidance vs graded-exposure for sport-required ranges** (CC-3, medium) — confirms the need for the
   graded-exposure allele.
3. **Under-integration of fear as the limiter** (CC-6, low–medium) — MCG↔D6 seam.

## Most unstable reasoning areas
- **SELECT recall (0.71)** — deciding *when to individualize/caution vs when to just load* is the least
  stable boundary. The model errs safe (good for safety, costly for progress).
- Notably, **zero false negatives on safety** — the instability is *over*-caution, never *under*-caution.
  For a safety domain, that is the correct direction to be wrong in.

**Verdict for D3:** the safety-critical behavior (red-flag routing, refusing to load through pain) is
**rock-solid (recall 1.00)**. The defect is *conservatism bias* on healthy athletes — a benign failure
direction, addressed by the asymptomatic fast-path and the graded-exposure allele.
