# Validation Pack 7 — Coaching Communication (Winkelman lineage, WNK)

**Mind Graph:** know the receiver → pick ONE intention to change → encode as a short, external, vivid
cue/analogy → deliver → observe the produced movement → (worked? reduce feedback, consolidate : change
the cue in their language) → design constraints when words fail. **Classes:** `ONE-EXTERNAL-CUE` ·
`REDUCE-COGNITIVE` (fewer words / analogy for overload) · `RE-ENCODE` (cue failed → re-phrase in their
terms) · `FADE-CONSTRAINT` (fade feedback / use task constraints) · `LEARNER-FIT` (scale to novice↔expert).

## Fully-worked confusion cases

**CC-1 · V-WNK-06 / 47 — rehab / expert needs internal focus (◐).** External focus is the correct default,
but rehab activation and some expert proprioceptive work genuinely need **brief internal cues**. *Failure:*
the graph applies external-focus universally. *Missing pattern:* the **internal-focus allele** (SPRINT-002
flagged it) — validation confirms the graph under-fires it. *Candidate update:* branch on context
(rehab/activation/expert-proprioception → internal allele).

**CC-2 · V-WNK-09 / 44 — safety-critical correction (◐).** Cue economy says "one short external cue," but an
**urgent safety fault** (bar drifting, bad spotter setup) warrants an immediate, direct, explicit
instruction regardless of elegance. *Failure:* economy over-prioritized over urgency. *Candidate update:*
a safety exception to cue-economy — directness beats economy when risk is imminent.

**CC-3 · V-WNK-25 — learner in pain during the drill (✗, missed risk).** The communication graph tries to
**re-encode the cue** when it should **stop and route to D3**. *Why fail:* no pain/safety interrupt above
the cueing loop. *Candidate update:* the same **top-level safety-interrupt** missing across Helms/Clear/
Gervais — communication must yield to safety. Cross-expert recurrence (5th lineage to show it).

**CC-4 · V-WNK-33 — anxious learner (◐).** Reduces cognitive load (right) but the primary limiter is
**state/anxiety (D6)** — the graph should sequence *regulate-then-cue* (Gervais CC/ordering). *Candidate
update:* couple with D6 regulate-first ordering. (This is the mirror of Gervais's under-integration of cueing.)

2. **No safety exception to cue economy / no pain interrupt** (CC-2/3, severity high — includes 1

## Most unstable reasoning areas
- **LEARNER-FIT recall (0.71 predicted, but really the CUE/internal boundary)** — deciding external vs
  internal, and economy vs directness, is WNK's least stable seam. Everything in the pure "make one
  external cue land and fade it" loop is highly stable (REDUCE/FADE recall 1.00).

**Verdict for D7:** strong (class-accuracy 0.94); the external-focus gene is correctly **polymorphic**
(needs the internal allele), and it shares the universal **safety-interrupt** gap. Confirms the
regulate-then-cue ordering with D6.
